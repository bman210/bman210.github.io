Humidity

The graph above looks at the relative humidity by city in comparison to latitude. Although the data seems to be random in nature most cities with high humidity fall between the latitudes of -20 to 20 and 40 to 60 degrees. The first group of cities between -20 to 20 degrees of latitude makes sense to have higher humidity because of its proximity to the equator. Being close to water and having high temperatures allows for more water to saturate the air. Having a high humidity between 40 to 60 degrees might surprise some, but these areas have high precipitation levels due to the water cycle. The heat from the equator evaporates a lot of water and as it makes its way up into the atmosphere it cools and precipitates back down to these areas.
